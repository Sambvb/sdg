Smart Energy Management Systems

Key Issues in Traditional Energy Systems

  • Energy Waste: Inefficient distribution causes waste and excessive energy use during non-peak times.
  • Unpredictable Costs: Fluctuating prices and lack of real-time data prevent optimal energy use.
  • Environmental Impact: Conventional energy sources increase carbon emissions, and poor networks lead to more energy loss.

Optimization Algorithms

  • Dijkstra's Algorithm: Purpose: Finds efficient energy routes, minimizing loss and cost.
  • Reinforcement Learning: Purpose: Adjusts energy consumption based on real-time data to match demand.
  • Floyd-Warshall Algorithm: Purpose: Optimizes overall energy distribution by calculating the shortest paths across the network.
  • Greedy Algorithms: Purpose: Balances energy during peak demand for efficiency.
  • Kruskal’s & Prim’s Algorithms: Purpose: Builds efficient network connections, reducing infrastructure costs.

Advantages

  • Reduced Waste: Efficient delivery avoids overuse.
  • Environmental Benefits: Optimized routing and renewable energy reduce emissions.
  • Cost Savings: Better scheduling and dynamic pricing lower costs for both providers and consumers.
  • Improved Reliability: Adaptive algorithms ensure stable supply, even during peak demand.

Dijkstra's Algorithm for Optimizing Energy Routes

Dijkstra’s Algorithm finds the shortest path from a power source to consumers, reducing energy loss.

  • Nodes: Power plants, substations, and consumers.
  • Edges: Energy transmission paths.
  • Weight: Energy loss, cost, or time.

How It Works: It calculates the best route to minimize energy loss.

Floyd-Warshall Algorithm for Load Balancing

Floyd-Warshall Algorithm finds the shortest paths between all network nodes, optimizing energy distribution.

  • Nodes: Power plants, substations, consumers.
  • Edges: Transmission lines.

How It Works: It balances energy supply and demand across the grid to avoid overloads.
